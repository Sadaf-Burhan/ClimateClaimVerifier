Week 5 · Fine-tuning with adapters

The adapter that shipped disabled negative result

Treating class balance as a hyperparameter turned "fine-tuning didn't help" into a mapped frontier: no ratio cleared recall ≥ 0.90 and precision ≥ 0.85 at once. So the adapter ships disabled — the base's recall-first errors get filtered downstream by evidence anyway. The failure has a shape, and that shape is the finding.

[3:00–3:35] — ~35s. The best slide in the deck. Own it.

Recall was solved; precision — about 0.70 — was the open problem, concentrated in vague-conspiracy posts over-called as claims. I couldn't fix it with a prompt rule: pushing precision that way sent it to 0.94 but collapsed recall to 0.69. So I went to a precision-targeted QLoRA — Option B.

The data was hybrid. The vague-versus-specific conspiracy boundary is exactly where a teacher model can't be trusted, so those ~26 examples are hand-labelled; the bulk came from a qwen2.5:7b teacher — deliberately stronger than the 3B student, and audited. I rejected gemma2:2b — it called 87% of everything a claim, contradicting its own reasons — and gemma4 ran out of memory on the T4. An in_train_set flag keeps training data out of the eval set — no leakage.

The move that made the week worth anything: I treated class balance as a hyperparameter and swept it — 26, 40, 50 percent claim. At 26% precision is beautiful, 0.95, but recall collapses to 0.81. At 50% recall is 0.96 and precision falls to 0.74. Nothing cleared both gates. Every recall-≥-0.90 point caps precision near 0.74; every precision-≥-0.85 point drops recall below 0.82. That's a real frontier at this model size and data volume — not label noise.

[click] So the adapter ships disabled — a Base-versus-Adapter demo tab, not the production model. And that's fine, because the architecture is recall-first: an opinion the base over-calls as a claim simply drops out downstream when the GDELT evidence doesn't corroborate it. Fine-tuning wasn't a failed week — it proved precision isn't a model problem, which is exactly why Week 6 solves it with evidence.
