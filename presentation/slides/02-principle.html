The focus

Two main things became the project focus

Recall‑first. A higher recall is the real target as a missed claim is unrecoverable. Precision can be improved downstream with better evidence retrieval.

Evidence, not verdicts. The system retrieves evidence and trust signals — it does not assign truth; evidence supports verification.

Recall — FN: How many claims falsely labelled as opinions.

Two things became the focus of the whole project.

[click] Recall-first. Missing a claim can't be recovered later, so the classifier is tuned to catch every potentially verifiable claim — even at the cost of false positives. Precision is recovered downstream, by retrieving evidence.

[click] Evidence, not verdicts. The system retrieves evidence and trust signals; it never assigns truth. The evidence supports the reader's own verification.

The footer defines the two metrics I lean on all deck: recall is how many real claims we caught — missing one is unrecoverable; precision is how many of the posts we flag as claims are actually claims — the rest are false alarms.

Keep these in mind — recall-first is why the fine-tuning week produced a negative result, and why the RAG evidence layer exists at all.
