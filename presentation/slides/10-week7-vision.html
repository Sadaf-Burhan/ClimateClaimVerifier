[4:05–4:40] — ~35s. Week 7 is multimodal: one model, two entry points.

The first entry point is gated vision on Bluesky posts. Every post now ingests its image, but the vision model only fires on ~1% — genuine edge cases where an image-claim isn't already resolved by metadata. There, a real on-the-ground photo can rescue a post and cartoon imagery reinforces suspicion. Its job is a small precision recovery on false positives — a documented ~1% supplement, not the workhorse.

The second entry point is the one I'm most excited about: an off-platform screenshot. You can paste a Bluesky link, or upload a screenshot of a Facebook, Instagram, or X post. That matters because misinformation mostly lives off Bluesky — this opens the scanner to a far wider audience. A screenshot runs through extract_from_image — a 9-field schema that transcribes what's legible, infers platform from branding, and nulls the rest, never guessing — then a thin adapter hands it to the same assess_claim pipeline. The spine is unchanged; the only downstream change is one source guard that treats an uploaded screenshot as unverified social.

And I'm honest about the tradeoff, because this is a trust tool. A screenshot strips exactly the data the signal is built on — no follower count, no verification — and adds lossy OCR and hallucination risk. So the screenshot path is explicitly labeled a lower-confidence signal: wider reach, weaker evidence, and I say so.

Also this week, two reader-signal reframes: a first-person eyewitness post and a forecast about a future event both get reframed rather than red-flagged for lacking news corroboration — with a conspiracy guard so "I saw them spraying chemtrails" still flags.
