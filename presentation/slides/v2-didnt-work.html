What didn't work — and the honest limits

Three walls I hit, and what each one taught

1 · A LoRA adapter couldn't buy precision. QLoRA (rank 8, Unsloth; 26 boundary cases + a qwen2.5:7b teacher), class balance swept as a hyperparameter:

Configrecallprecision
Base, single-post shipped0.9380.703
Adapter · 26% claim0.8120.951
Adapter · 40% claim0.8540.788
Adapter · 50% claim0.9580.742

No ratio clears recall ≥ 0.90 and precision ≥ 0.85 at once. A real frontier, not label noise → adapter ships disabled, base stays recall-first. The failure has a shape, and the shape is the finding.

2 · Embeddings alone can't corroborate. Dense similarity conflates topic with truth: a HAARP conspiracy post scores 0.62 against real weather news — vocabulary overlap, not evidence. So the verdict can only ever say "partial," never "corroborated" — proximity is a relevance signal, not a fact-check.

3 · The corpus is headline-only. GDELT gives titles, not article bodies. The fix for wall #2 — an LLM re-rank / verification pass — earns its keep on full passages; over bare headlines it reads almost the same tokens the bi-encoder already saw, so it wasn't worth shipping. Event-level confirmation would need full-text fetch + re-rank — deferred, not pretended.

Together these are exactly why the system reports signals, not verdicts — and they set up the next-steps slide.

~1 min — the reflection / tradeoffs beat. Three honest walls. (1) LoRA: I tried to buy precision and the table shows I couldn't clear both gates — so I shipped it disabled, and that negative result is real evidence, not a gap I'm hiding. (2) Embeddings conflate topic with truth (HAARP 0.62), so I never claim "corroborated." (3) The deeper cause: headline-only GDELT means a re-ranker has little extra signal to exploit — the honest bottleneck. All three are why the whole system is signals-not-verdicts, and they hand straight off to the future-work slide.
