The problem

Language models are designed to reason over text; they do not possess an independent mechanism for verifying factual claims. ClimateClaimVerifier retrieves evidence and trust signals to support informed human judgment.

Where the LLM actually helps — most of Climate Scanner is deliberately not an LLM: the embeddings, retrieval, red-flag rules and source allow-lists are classical, so they can't hallucinate. The LLM is confined to the two jobs a database can't do — (1) judging whether a sentence even contains a checkable claim, telling “they're spraying chemtrails, wake up” (checkable → flag) from “this weather is insane lol” (just a feeling), at high recall; and (2) reading an arbitrary screenshot into structured fields (claim, platform, author, engagement) where plain OCR yields only characters. Could it run without an LLM? A cruder skeleton could — keyword rules over the same retrieval — but recall would collapse exactly where misinformation hides (sarcasm, checkable-but-false conspiracy), the off-platform image path would vanish, and you'd trade a prompt + 8 examples for a labelled corpus and a training pipeline. So the LLM reads and classifies; retrieval and rules attach the evidence; you form the verdict.

[0:15–1:00] — the problem, ~45s

Climate misinformation spreads faster than fact-checkers can work. A reader scrolling past a viral post has about three seconds and three real questions: is this even a checkable claim? did anyone actually report this event? and who's telling me?

The tempting build is an LLM that stamps TRUE or FALSE, or emits a credibility score. This was not considered due to the limitations of LLMs A 3-billion-parameter model has no ability to verify a fact — it only has an ability to sound confident. And a credibility table — is a curation and bias burden you have to defend forever.
