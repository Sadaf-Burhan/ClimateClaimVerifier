The problem

Language models are designed to reason over text; they do not possess an independent mechanism for verifying factual claims. ClimateClaimVerifier retrieves evidence and trust signals to support informed human judgment.

[0:15–1:00] — the problem, ~45s

Climate misinformation spreads faster than fact-checkers can work. A reader scrolling past a viral post has about three seconds and three real questions: is this even a checkable claim? did anyone actually report this event? and who's telling me?

The tempting build is an LLM that stamps TRUE or FALSE, or emits a credibility score. This was not considered due to the limitations of LLMs A 3-billion-parameter model has no ability to verify a fact — it only has an ability to sound confident. And a credibility table — is a curation and bias burden you have to defend forever.

So: metal detector, not judge. It beeps. It doesn't tell you what you dug up. That constraint is the spine of the whole system.
