Here's the whole system, top to bottom.

Data sources — Bluesky posts and GDELT climate news — feed the ingestion pipeline, which runs on my laptop every 24 hours: collect, filter with the non-LLM topic filter, normalize, embed, store. That fills the shared data layer — a database of posts and labels, and a vector store of news evidence.

From there it splits. The classification engine runs the heavy LLM work on a Colab GPU — claim detection, image understanding, and the maintenance and admin-relabel pass. The reader application is the user-facing entry, taking a Bluesky link, a post from the feed, or an off-platform screenshot.

Both funnel into the one assessment engine — retrieve evidence, corroborate the source, build trust signals, detect red flags — which produces the user report. Notice the bottom line, in red: supporting evidence, sources, signals, flags — and no truth verdict. That's the whole philosophy in one box. And notice there's no LoRA: it was measured and left out.
