[3:30–4:05] — ~35s. Where precision actually gets solved.

Week 6 recovers precision — downstream, exactly as Week 5 predicted. I built a ChromaDB store of GDELT news, embedded with all-MiniLM in cosine space, carrying the same leakage guard from earlier weeks — eval and train posts are excluded. For a claim, dense top-k retrieval pulls the nearest articles.

The key insight: pure dense similarity conflates topic with corroboration. A HAARP conspiracy post scores 0.62 against real weather news just for sharing the topic. That's vocabulary overlap, not evidence.

The fix is region-aware retrieval. I embed location into both sides, the article and the query, so same-region news ranks higher. And crucially, the verdict comes from retrieval proximity: a strong match reads "partial," never "corroborated." The system deliberately never claims full corroboration from a headline — it stays conservative.

[click] Two red-flag guards close the loop: a post that cites its own credible source, or comes from an official account, is never flagged. Throughout, this stays a relevance signal, never a truth verdict — it surfaces whether published news covers the same event, and the reader concludes.
