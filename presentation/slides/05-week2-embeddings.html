[1:50–2:15] — ~25s. This is a "reasons behind the choice" slide.

Week 2 is the semantic layer — turn text into a vector, compare by cosine similarity. To choose a model I built a domain-pair eval — about twenty pairs that should be similar versus dissimilar — searched MTEB for the semantic-similarity task, and scored each model by its separation gap.

Two rejections worth stating. gte-small scored high on the similar pairs and also high on the dissimilar ones — it flags nearly everything as similar, a word-level-versus-sentence-level mismatch, so it can't discriminate. And the course baseline, nomic-embed-text, is a long-context model — an advantage my data doesn't use.

[click] Here's the actual reasoning. My text is short — Bluesky posts and GDELT gives me headlines only — so long context is wasted and the real job is semantic similarity on short text. That makes all-MiniLM-L6-v2 the right pick: it's fast, runs fully offline with no second inference server, 384 dimensions are plenty, and — the clincher — the same encoder becomes the Week 6 RAG retrieval engine. all-mpnet had a slightly wider separation gap but it's 768-dim and heavier — overkill here. Validated at a 0.43 gap: similar pairs 0.64, dissimilar 0.21.
